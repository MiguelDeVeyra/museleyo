The Negros Museum have been present since 1996 through the Negros Cultural Foundation Inc. Since then, the organization became the forefront of the region’s cultural and art landscape through its active programming such as in the form of exhibits/artist’s talks, interactive tours, workshops, lecture/training, performances, events and festivals. Aside from its Museum function, it also has upgraded its venue facilities with an expansion of its very own Cultural HUB that features multi - purpose and art spaces open as a hub for education, information-sharing, and collective reflection together with artists, art managers, cultural workers and educators and well as provide a venue for enhancing the skills of art enthusiasts and hobbyists of all ages. The Negros Museum also had strong partnerships and linkages with National Commission for Culture and the Arts, Cultural Center of the Philippines, Film Development Council of the Philippines, National Archives of the Philippines and Local Government Units in the Province.

The Negros Museum is the provincial museum of Negros Occidental and is considered as the home of Negrense stories as well as a hub for cultural and artistic activities and events.

It provides multi - functional spaces that are open to everyone who wish to engage in cultural, artistic and community based activities bringing together local-based artists, cultural workers involved in art making, art promotions, artistic collaborations and cultural programming, coming from different artistic disciplines to share their best practices with each other.

PROGRAMS

Exhibits and Artist Talks

The permanent and changing exhibits in the museum’s galleries are supplemented by artist talks to emphasize the creative process, inspiration, and exploration of the artist.

Interactive Tours

Ang mga Istorya ni Bao program caters to the museum’s primary target audience which are the children and youth, the museum education program developed an Educational Tour aimed at providing cultural enrichment to young audiences, in order to experiences, create, and learn in an educationally enjoyable environment that will engage young visitors and stimulate their imagination.

Workshops and Training

In partnership with cultural institutions, the museum conducts seminars and training to develop artists, art managers, cultural workers and educators and also provide a venue for enhancing the skills of art enthusiasts and hobbyists of all ages. The museum also holds an annual summer workshop to provide a venue for holistic development of the children and youth.

Performances and Festivals

The museum organizes intimate and large plays, concerts and festivals within the museum spaces and other performance spaces, as well as outreach tours to make the art experience accessible to a larger and varied audience.

PROJECTS

MUGNA

Towards A Sustained Cultural Development in Negros Island

MUGNA: Towards A Sustained Cultural Development in Negros Island which aims to effectively seed the foundations of a cultural development process with the purpose of establishing a pro-active cultural renaissance in selected towns of Negros Island.

Mugna has benefitted more than 6,000 participants from 10 selected LGU’s composed of government workers, teachers, students, craftsmen, artists, and general public who participated in different workshops, lectures, and exhibitions propelling genuine community-based productivity and development.

LIHOK

Arts Festival and Congress

LIHOK Arts Festival and Congress brought together the Visayas-based artists, organizers and cultural workers involved in art making, art promotions, artistic collaborations and cultural programming, coming from different artistic disciplines to share their best practices with each other. Among the festival components are the creative industry fair and exhibit, performances and educational tours and conference.

PANAKAYON

Promotion of Visayas Culture

PANAKAYON Promotion of Visayas Culture launched in 2016 utilized an online or digital platform to promote the rich Visayan culture, practices, traditions, history, and arts that have influenced our way of life.

Panakayon, a vernacular word for journey, was composed of virtual tour and photo essay competitions, the Pamana ni Lola webserye, an MTV on culture, online games and a 360 photo booth experience.

HANAS

Scholarship Program for Second Liners

In partnership with the West Visayas Association of Museums and the NCCA, the museum hosted the HANAS: Scholarship Program for Second Liners. Hanas is a platform which aspires to develop next generation of multidisciplinary artists and art managers while at the same time strengthening the cultural networks in the region.

  • PARTNERS

    CULTURAL CENTER OF THE PHILIPPINES

    The Cultural Center of the Philippines (CCP) is the premiere showcase of the arts in the Philippines. Founded in 1969, the CCP has been producing and presenting music, dance, theater, visual arts, literary, cinematic and design events from the Philippines and all over the world for more than forty years.

    For almost three decades, the Cultural Center of the Philippines has been a long-standing and staunch partner of the Negros Cultural Foundation and The Negros Museum. CCPs Outreach and Exchange Division has recognized the Negros Cultural Foundation as one of its Regional Arts Centers through its Kaisa sa Sining Program.

    This partnership has brought many collaborations between the foundation, the museum and the Cultural Center to include bringing to Bacolod and Negros performances of internationally and nationally renowned artists.

  • PARTNERS

    THE PERFORMANCE LABORATORY

    The Performance Laboratory, Inc. is a Negros based theatre-company dedicated to creating a space for theater artists to collaborate, exchange and strengthen their network and linkages with each other. The Performance Laboratory was loosely organized in 2002 by USLS Maskara Theatre Ensemble alumni.

  • PARTNERS

    FILM DEVELOPMENT COUNCIL OF THE PHILIPPINES

    The Film Development Council of the Philippines is the national film agency under the Office of the President of the Philippines responsible for film policies and programs to ensure the economic, cultural and educational development of the Philippine film industry.

    FDCP partnered with the museum to put up Cinematheque Negoros to serve as a venue for screening classic and contemporary Filipino films and a hub for film development programs and on-ground events that promote cultural exchange and nurture a community's film culture.

  • PARTNERS

    WESTERN VISAYAS ASSOCIATION OF MUSEUMS, INC.s

    The West Visayas Association of Museum, Inc main objective is to unite all museum and museum workers of Western Visayas promote conservation and preservation of cultural heritage; organize activities which address common concerns in museum issues as well as bring together museum work.
